1. The "100 Listing" Limit (Section 6.3b)
This is a technical restriction many developers miss.

The Change: Your search results or map view cannot allow a user to retrieve more than 100 listings in a single query/view.

Implementation: Cap your Supabase query with .limit(100). If you have a "Load More" button, ensure the cumulative results for that specific search session don't exceed 100.

2. Mandatory Display Requirements (Section 6.3c, e, i, k)
You need to update your UI components (Tailwind) to include specific labels.

Brokerage Credit: You must display the Listing Brokerage on every listing, including the small thumbnail/card views.

Constraint: It must be the same font and size as the price/address. You cannot hide it in tiny grey text.

Contact Separation: Your own contact info or "Book a Showing" button must be clearly separated from the listing details so users don't think the house is your listing (unless it actually is).

Mandatory Notices: You must add these two specific blocks of text to your site (footer or listing detail page):

"The information provided herein is deemed reliable but is not guaranteed accurate by PROPTX."

"The information provided herein must only be used by consumers that have a bona fide interest in the purchase, sale, or lease of real estate and may not be used for any commercial purpose or any other purpose."

3. Data Integrity & Refresh Rate (Section 6.3f, h)
No Alterations: You can't change the data. If the MLS says "3 Bedrooms," you can't display "3+0 Bedrooms" or "Luxury 3 Bed" in the data field. You can only reformat it (e.g., changing "3" to "3 Beds").

24-Hour Refresh: Your Supabase sync script must run at least once every 24 hours.

Tip: Set up a GitHub Action or a Vercel Cron Job to trigger your Bridge API sync daily.

4. The "AI Guardrails" (Section 6.2f, k)
This is a newer, very strict section. Since you are into "vibe coding" and AI orchestration, pay close attention:

No AI Training: You are strictly prohibited from feeding IDX data into an AI System to "produce content" or "extract patterns."

What this means for you: You can use AI to write the code for your site, but you cannot send the listing descriptions or data to an LLM (like GPT-4 or Claude) to "summarize the listing" or "generate a marketing blurb" for the user.

The Change: Ensure your data flow doesn't pipe listing text into an AI API for front-end display.

5. Security & Anti-Scraping (Section 6.2d, g)
Because you are hosting on Vercel/Supabase, you are responsible for the data's safety.

The Change: Implement basic rate-limiting to prevent others from scraping your site.

Implementation: Use Vercel’s Web Application Firewall (WAF) or a middleware layer in Next.js to block excessive requests. PROPTX holds you liable if a third party scrapes the data from tourit.ca.

6. Pre-Approval (Section 6.3g)
The Change: Before you go live or change your URL (e.g., from a dev domain to tourit.ca), you must get your Managing Broker of Record at Bay Street Realty to approve the URL in the PROPTX Online Agreement System.
